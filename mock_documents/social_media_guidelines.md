# Social Media Guidelines

## Purpose

Community Support Organization uses social media to share public updates, community education, volunteer opportunities, and general stories about the organization's work.

## Drafting Posts

Draft posts should use clear, respectful, and welcoming language. Posts should avoid jargon, unsupported claims, and details that could identify a client, donor, volunteer, or staff member without permission.

## Approval Workflow

Social media posts must be reviewed before publishing. Drafts are usually reviewed by the communications lead or another assigned approver. Posts about programs, events, partnerships, or sensitive topics may require additional review.

## Consent for Photos and Stories

Photos, quotes, and personal stories require documented consent before they are published. Consent should be specific to the intended use whenever possible. If consent is unclear, the content should not be posted.

## Avoiding Identifiable Client Details

Posts must not include identifiable client details, private service information, case notes, personal safety information, or other confidential information. When sharing impact examples, use broad, anonymous language.

## Brand Tone

The organization's public tone should be warm, accurate, inclusive, and grounded. Social media should not exaggerate outcomes, pressure people to disclose personal experiences, or present AI-generated content as verified fact without review.
