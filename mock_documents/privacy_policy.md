# Privacy Policy

## Purpose

Community Support Organization handles personal information with care, respect, and accountability. This fictional policy explains how staff and volunteers should protect private information while using internal systems and AI-assisted tools.

## Handling Personal Information

Personal information includes names, contact details, appointment notes, donor records, volunteer records, staff information, and any details that could identify a person. Staff and volunteers should collect only the information needed for an approved purpose.

Private information must be stored only in approved internal systems. Files should not be copied into personal drives, personal email accounts, or unapproved apps.

## Confidentiality Expectations

Staff and volunteers are expected to keep client, donor, volunteer, and staff information confidential. Confidential details should be discussed only with people who need the information to complete their assigned work.

When sharing information internally, use the minimum amount of detail needed. Avoid including unnecessary personal details in summaries, reports, or meeting notes.

## AI Tool Restrictions

Identifiable client, donor, volunteer, or staff data must not be pasted into public AI tools. Public AI tools include consumer chatbots, public writing assistants, and any tool that has not been reviewed and approved by the organization.

Staff may use AI tools only for low-risk tasks such as drafting general templates, summarizing anonymous survey themes, or improving non-confidential wording. Sensitive work must remain in approved internal systems.

## No Identifiable Client Data in Public AI Tools

Do not enter real names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, case notes, medical details, immigration status, personal safety information, or other confidential information into public AI tools.

If a task requires examples, use fictional examples or fully anonymized text that cannot identify any person.

## Human Review Requirements

AI-generated content must be reviewed by a human before it is used. Staff are responsible for checking accuracy, tone, privacy, and policy alignment.

AI tools must not be used as the final decision-maker for service eligibility, safety planning, disciplinary actions, complaints, or other sensitive decisions.

## Approved Internal Systems Only

Documents containing confidential information should be stored, updated, and shared only through approved internal systems. If staff are unsure whether a tool is approved, they should ask their supervisor or the privacy lead before using it.
