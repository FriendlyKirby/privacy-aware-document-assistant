# Incident Reporting Policy

## Purpose

Community Support Organization uses incident reporting to respond to safety concerns, service disruptions, privacy concerns, and other unexpected events.

## Report Incidents Internally

Staff and volunteers should report incidents through the approved internal reporting process as soon as practical. The report should be factual, timely, and limited to information needed for review and follow-up.

## Urgent Escalation

Urgent safety concerns should be escalated immediately to the appropriate supervisor or emergency contact according to internal procedures. Staff should not wait to complete a written report before escalating an urgent issue.

## Documentation Expectations

Incident documentation should include what happened, when it happened, where it happened, who was notified, and what immediate action was taken. Reports should avoid speculation and should distinguish observed facts from follow-up questions.

## Avoid Unnecessary Personal Details

Incident reports should not include unnecessary personal details. Include only the information needed to understand the incident, support follow-up, and meet internal documentation requirements.

## Do Not Use Public AI Tools

Sensitive incident reports must not be copied into public AI tools. Staff should not use public AI tools to rewrite, summarize, or analyze incident reports that contain confidential or identifiable information.
