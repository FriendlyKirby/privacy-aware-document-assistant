# Volunteer Onboarding

## Purpose

Community Support Organization welcomes volunteers through a structured onboarding process. The process helps volunteers understand their role, safety expectations, confidentiality responsibilities, and available support.

## Application

Prospective volunteers complete an application form that includes contact information, availability, areas of interest, and relevant experience. Applications are reviewed by the volunteer coordinator.

## Screening

Screening may include a short interview, reference checks, and role-specific checks when required. Screening requirements depend on the level of access, the nature of the volunteer role, and whether the role involves direct service.

## Orientation

Approved volunteers attend an orientation session before beginning their role. Orientation covers the organization's mission, basic procedures, communication expectations, and boundaries for volunteer work.

## Confidentiality Agreement

Volunteers must sign a confidentiality agreement before accessing internal information or participating in service activities. Volunteers are reminded not to share client, donor, volunteer, or staff details outside approved settings.

## Training

Volunteers complete role-specific training before working independently. Training may include service procedures, respectful communication, incident reporting, privacy basics, and safe use of internal systems.

## Supervisor Check-In

Each volunteer is assigned a supervisor or main contact. The supervisor provides guidance, answers questions, reviews early experiences, and confirms when the volunteer is ready for regular scheduling.
